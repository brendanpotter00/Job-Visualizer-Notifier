How to read this — methodology & honest caveats

  • Onboarding exclusion is everything. 128 of 131 boards were imported in the May 17–20 wave, so a company's first day is its whole board, not new demand. All "new/surge/momentum" figures use last-7/14-day windows, which sit ~3 weeks clear of that wave.
  • first_seen_at ≠ true posting date. It's when your scraper first saw a listing (posted_on is ~10% null and unused). Treat all dates as observation timestamps.
  • Turnaround is left/right-censored. Jobs alive at onboarding are excluded (age unknown); long-lived May-cohort jobs haven't closed yet, biasing "fastest" low. The 17.1-day median is a floor.
  • Freezes are validated, not assumed. Each mass-closure day is cross-checked against scrape volume & error count. One false freeze (Google 05-05) and one fake surge cluster (quant firms) were caught and removed.
  • Remote is undercounted. Only ~25,772 open jobs carry a normalized primary location, and is_remote_eligible is null for ~6,400 — location-restricted cuts miss remote roles.
  • Read-only analysis. No writes to production. Generated from job_listings, scrape_runs, job_locations, locations, companies.
